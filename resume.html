Summary

 

Tanaka Chingonzo has diverse experience in innovative social impact projects, web design, and development. He has served in the Zimbabwe National Children's Parliament, a Chief Executive Officer in an incubator company under the Junior Achievement International initiative and as an Interact club president under

Rotary International. He spent a semester at Watson University, Boulder, Colorado working on The Pamwe Foundation to help communities support their cities in providing better service. Tanaka also interned at Spark Boulder- a co-working space for entrepreneurs in the Denver-Boulder area. He volunteered with The Vinetta Project, providing a support network for female entrepreneurs assisting them with access to funding and mentorship in excess of $13 million in seed investment venture capital. He is a member of the Global Engagement Summit network which he participated in at the 2016 summit at Northwestern University in

Chicago. He currently develops websites for social ventures and runs Apply.co.zw while interning at Education

USA- at the US Embassy's public affairs section.

January 2016 - Present

I raised $3500+ to solve infrastructure problems in cities. Cities across Zimbabwe have poor infrastructure due to the populations rapid growth at 3% annually. The poor infrastructure is worsened by a lack of governmental intervention. Failing infrastructure causes road traffic accidents, poses health risks and crime. Failing infrastructure is not a result of shortcomings in the governments budget. It is a result of the government's lack of awareness about where these problems are occurring around the city. The Pamwe Foundation is working to reduce this.
